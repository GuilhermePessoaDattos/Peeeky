## Introduction
In the world of fundraising, having a well-organized and easily accessible virtual data room (VDR) is crucial. It not only streamlines the due diligence process but also creates a professional impression on potential investors. In this guide, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of setting up a virtual data room for fundraising using Peeeky, an easy-to-use and secure platform designed for this purpose.

## What is a Virtual Data Room?
A virtual data room is an online repository used to store and share sensitive documents securely. Primarily utilized during fundraising rounds, it allows startups to provide potential investors with access to critical information they need to evaluate the opportunity without compromising security.

## Step 1: Determine Your Document Needs
Before diving into the setup, define which documents you will need in the VDR. Common documents for fundraising include:
- **Business Plan**: Outline your business model, market analysis, and revenue projections.
- **Financial Statements**: Include past financial statements and future projections to give investors a clear picture of your financial health.
- **Corporate Governance Documents**: These may include articles of incorporation, bylaws, and board meeting minutes.
- **Intellectual Property Information**: Patents, trademarks, and copyrights should be documented.
- **Market Research**: Any reports or studies that validate your business idea and target market.

## Step 3: Create Your Virtual Data Room
After logging in, follow these steps to create your VDR:
1. **Navigate to the VDR Section**: Find the “Documents” or “Data Rooms” tab in the main menu.
2. **Create a New Data Room**: Click on the “Create Data Room” button, and fill in the necessary details, such as the name and description of the data room. Make sure to include key terms like "fundraising data room" to help you keep it organized.
3. **Set Permissions**: Choose who can access your data room and what permissions they have. You can set specific access rights for investors, team members, or advisors.

## Step 4: Upload Your Documents
Now that your data room is created, it’s time to upload the documents you gathered in Step 1. Here’s how:
1. **Drag and Drop or Upload**: You can either drag and drop documents into the data room or use the upload button to select files from your computer.
2. **Organize Your Files**: Create folders for different categories of documents (e.g., Financials, Corporate Governance, Market Research) to make navigation easier for viewers.
3. **Set Document Permissions**: For sensitive documents, consider restricting access or creating view-only links.

## Step 5: Customize Your Data Room
Once your documents are uploaded, take some time to customize the data room:
1. **Branding**: Add your company logo and colors to create a professional look.
2. **Landing Page**: Customize the landing page to include a brief overview of your company and purpose of the fundraising.
3. **Notifications**: Set up notifications to keep track of who accesses the documents and when.

## Step 6: Share Your Data Room with Investors
Now that your VDR is ready, it’s time to share it with potential investors:
1. **Invite Users**: Send invitations to investors by entering their email addresses directly from the Peeeky dashboard.
2. **Generate a Shareable Link**: Alternatively, you can generate a shareable link for your data room. Make sure to set expiration dates or limits on access to maintain security.

## Step 7: Monitor and Manage Your Data Room
As investors begin to access your virtual data room, keep track of the engagement:
1. **View Access Logs**: Peeeky provides analytics to help you see who has accessed the data room and which documents they viewed.
2. **Respond to Questions**: Be prompt in responding to any queries from investors that may arise from their review of the documents.
3. **Update Documents**: Regularly update your VDR with new data or documents as your fundraising progresses.

## Step 8: Close the Round
Once your fundraising efforts are completed, you may want to archive or delete the data room:
1. **Archive**: Keep archived records for future reference, especially if you plan to continue fundraising down the line.
2. **Delete**: Ensure you delete any sensitive information if the round has concluded and you do not wish for it to remain accessible.
