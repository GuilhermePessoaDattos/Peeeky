## Proposed Response

A few layers to this depending on what you're actually trying to track:

**For website behavior:** PostHog is the go-to for most indie hackers right now — open source, free up to a generous limit, session recordings + funnels + feature flags all in one. Plausible or Fathom if you want lighter-weight and privacy-friendly.

**One layer most founders skip entirely:** document analytics. If you're sharing pitch decks, investor one-pagers, sales proposals, or product spec sheets through email, you're essentially flying blind on what happens after the send — did they open it, which sections did they actually read, did they forward it to someone else. Website analytics is the front door; document analytics is what happens inside the conversations those visitors turn into.

I was surprised how much this changed my own follow-up behavior. Knowing that an investor spent 8 minutes on the traction slide but 15 seconds on the team slide tells you something specific about what to reinforce in the follow-up email. Generic "just checking in" notes feel different when you actually know what they engaged with.

Tools in this space range from DocSend (well-known, but expensive and per-user pricing gets painful) to open-source options like Papermark, to newer tools like Peeeky that layer in AI Q&A so the recipient can ask questions directly to the document — and those questions come back to you as engagement signal. None of them replace website analytics but they plug a real blind spot in the outbound side of distribution.

What's the specific thing you're trying to measure — acquisition, activation, or something in your outbound/sales flow?
