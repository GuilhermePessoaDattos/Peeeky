## Proposed Response

This is a fantastic resource — the gap between "I studied 50 funded pitch decks" and "I understand what made investors say yes" is usually in the narrative flow and sequencing, not the design polish. Having a categorized database at this scale helps close that.

One thing I'd add for founders using this to prep their own decks: the deck itself is only half the battle. Once you share it, *how* you send it matters a lot. A few things that have meaningfully improved response rates for founders:

- Share a tracked link, not a PDF attachment. Knowing an investor opened your deck at 8am on a Sunday (and came back Tuesday) tells you exactly when to follow up and how warm they are.
- Page-level time data tells you which slide landed and which one lost them — you can iterate your deck based on real behavioral data rather than guessing.
- Some newer tools (including Peeeky, which we're building) let the recipient chat with the document via AI — so investors can ask "how does this compare to [competitor]?" directly in the deck view. The questions they ask are gold for your follow-up pitch.

Congrats on the 2.0 launch — the categorization by industry vertical is particularly useful.
