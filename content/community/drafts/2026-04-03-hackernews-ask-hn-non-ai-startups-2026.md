## Context

Real HN thread posted approximately April 1, 2026, asking whether any founders are building without AI at the core. Multiple founders responded with their non-AI or minimally-AI projects: NGO fundraising platforms, API clients, ATS tools, hardware manufacturing, NAS applications. The thread is a genuine community check-in on the AI fatigue/hype dynamic. Relevant for Peeeky in two ways: (1) Peeeky is a document sharing and analytics tool where AI is a meaningful but not gimmicky addition — worth noting the distinction, and (2) this is a high-engagement HN thread where a substantive, self-aware founder perspective will get upvotes. The right approach: comment authentically about building something where AI solves a real problem rather than being a positioning exercise, without turning it into product promotion.

## Proposed Response

Building document sharing and analytics — the core value (knowing who opened your pitch deck, which pages they spent time on, whether they forwarded it) has nothing to do with AI and everything to do with solving a genuine information asymmetry for founders and sales reps.

We do have an AI layer: recipients can chat with the document instead of emailing questions back to the sender, and those conversations surface back to the sender as richer engagement signals than page dwell time alone. But it's a real use case — replacing "email me if you have questions" with a conversational interface that actually works on a static PDF — not AI as decoration.

The honest tension I've noticed in 2026: "AI-powered" as a positioning claim has become so diluted it's nearly meaningless in B2B SaaS. If your AI feature is just GPT-4 with a thin wrapper, users figure that out within a session. The things that hold up are AI solving problems that couldn't have been solved without it (document Q&A without requiring human availability is genuinely new) versus AI solving problems that were already solved adequately (AI-generated proposal summaries when the proposal was already readable).

Happy to see more founders in here who are asking this question. The pressure to add "AI-native" to your pitch deck regardless of product reality is real — sometimes it helps fundraising, sometimes it just creates debt you have to manage later when the feature doesn't earn its place.
