## Proposed Response

Really interesting problem to work on — the signal quality difference between "did they open it" vs "which pages held attention" is massive and most founders don't realize it until they've sent 20 decks into the void.

A few things from the fundraising analytics space that might be useful for your product thinking:

**The data that actually changes founder behavior:**
1. Time-per-page (obvious, but powerful — a VC who spent 45 seconds on your market size slide cares about TAM; lead with that in your follow-up)
2. Whether the link was forwarded or re-opened from a different domain (signals the deck made it to a partner meeting or got shared internally — huge positive signal)
3. Drop-off point — which slide do people close on? Usually points to a specific messaging problem, and it's fixable
4. Return visits — someone who opens the deck three times over two days is warm; someone who opened once for 30 seconds is not

**What I'd be cautious about:** "engagement scores" that combine all signals into a single number feel clean but can mislead. A score of 72 doesn't tell you whether to call or email. The individual signals tell you *how* to follow up, not just *whether* to.

**On the AI angle:** The underexplored feature in this space is letting the recipient ask questions. Instead of a VC thinking "I wonder about their burn rate" and closing the deck, they can just ask — and you get visibility into their exact concern before the call. Changes the dynamic of the first meeting entirely.

Are you building this specifically for fundraising decks, or broader (sales proposals, client onboarding, etc.)? The use cases overlap a lot in terms of tracking tech but diverge on UX — investors want friction-free access, enterprise sales buyers often want downloadable PDFs for internal routing.

(Full disclosure: I work on Peeeky, which is in this space — happy to compare notes or share what we've learned about the hardest problems here.)
