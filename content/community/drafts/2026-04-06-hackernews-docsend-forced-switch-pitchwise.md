## Proposed Response

The Dropbox/DocSend pricing shift has clearly shaken a lot of people loose. A few things worth considering when evaluating document-tracking tools for fundraising specifically:

**What actually matters in the analytics:**
- Per-slide time — so you know which section of your deck loses investors (usually the financials or competitive landscape)
- Unique-link-per-investor tracking — sending one link to everyone makes it impossible to know who's actually warm
- Forwarding detection — when a partner is intrigued, they share with another partner before replying; that signal is gold
- Re-visit alerts — a second or third visit, days later, usually means internal discussion is happening

**Where most tools fall short:**
Most tools (including Pitchwise) give you the view data but leave you to interpret it manually. The high-signal workflow is: view alert fires → you check which investor, how long, which slides → you follow up within the same business day while you're still top of mind.

If you're in active fundraising, the tool choice matters less than having a per-investor link discipline. Create a unique link for each firm, label it something readable (e.g., `/deck/sequoia`), and you get clean attribution without any CRM integration.

For what it's worth, I've been using Peeeky (peeeky.com) for exactly this — it's a DocSend alternative that does per-page analytics and per-recipient links. Still early but it solves the core loop well. Happy to share more if useful, though the main advice stands regardless of tool.
