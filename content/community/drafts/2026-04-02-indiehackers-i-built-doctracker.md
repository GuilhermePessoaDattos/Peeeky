## Proposed Response

Really cool that you shipped this — the document tracking space has a lot of surface area that's still genuinely underbuilt.

A few things I've noticed from being in this category that might be useful as you figure out where to take it:

**The "who opened it" problem vs. the "what did they think" problem:** Most tracking tools solve the first one well — you know when, for how long, on which pages. But the signal founders and sales reps actually want most is *what the reader was uncertain about*. The gap between "they spent 4 minutes on page 8" and "they wanted to ask about the pricing model but couldn't" is pretty significant for conversion. The tools that bridge that (making documents conversational so recipients can ask questions, and surfacing those questions as engagement data for the sender) have a differentiation story that's hard to copy with basic analytics.

**ICP matters a lot here:** Document tracking tools tend to spread thin across founders, sales teams, M&A advisors, and consultants — but each persona has very different workflows and willingness to pay. We've seen the clearest retention from use cases where the stakes per document are high (fundraising decks, enterprise proposals, due diligence packages) because those users feel the pain acutely.

**The cold start problem:** Getting someone to share a doc through your link instead of just attaching a PDF to an email is actually a non-trivial habit change. What's your current strategy for activation — what event in a user's first session tells you they've really "got it"?

Genuine curiosity — what made you build this specifically? Was there a personal pain point?

(For context: I'm building in adjacent territory with Peeeky, so partly asking as a peer trying to understand the problem space better.)
