## Proposed Response

Three gaps I've seen come up repeatedly when talking to founders going through a raise:

**1. No feedback loop on what was actually read.**
Founders send a deck via email attachment or a Google Drive link, and then they're flying blind. Did the partner open it? Did they forward it to the general partner? Spent three minutes on the financial model or five seconds? You're making follow-up decisions with zero signal. Tracked links (DocSend-style) solve this, but DocSend is now $45+/user/month and the free tier is gone, so a lot of early-stage founders are back to attachments.

**2. CRM tools don't talk to document analytics.**
You track your pipeline in Notion or Airtable or a spreadsheet, then your deck engagement lives in a completely separate tool (if it lives anywhere at all). Nobody has cleanly stitched these two together at the early-stage price point. The best workaround I've seen is a Zapier bridge between a document-sharing tool and a lightweight CRM, but that's a lot of configuration overhead for a solo founder in the middle of a raise.

**3. No "version awareness" for iterating decks mid-raise.**
You update your deck after an investor meeting, re-send to four other firms — but earlier links you sent still point to v1. Or you're not sure which version different investors have seen. Tools that let you update the underlying document without breaking existing share links (and that log which version each viewer actually saw) seem like an obvious need nobody has fully nailed.

Happy to compare notes if you're building in this space or have found tools that address any of these.

*(Full disclosure: I'm building Peeeky, which tackles the tracked link + per-page analytics side of this problem. The CRM integration gap is real and something we're working on.)*
