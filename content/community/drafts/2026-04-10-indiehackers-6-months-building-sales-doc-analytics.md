## Proposed Response

Great writeup — this mirrors a lot of what we've seen building Peeeky. A few things that matched our experience and a couple that diverged:

**Segment stickiness:** We found investor relations (founders sharing pitch decks during a raise) had the fastest activation but the worst retention — it's inherently episodic use. The stickiest segment for us has been sales teams using it for proposals and demo follow-ups. They send documents multiple times a week and the follow-up notifications become part of their daily workflow. That's where the habit forms.

**The "already have DocSend" objection:** Most of the prospects we talk to have *tried* DocSend and either left when Dropbox jacked up pricing or are still on it but frustrated. The real objection isn't "we have a tool" — it's "we don't have a follow-up workflow built around document signals yet." That's the education job, not the feature comparison job.

**On pricing:** We learned quickly that per-seat pricing for small sales teams doesn't work — teams of 2-3 AEs churn fast when they're still deciding if document tracking matters to them. A usage-based model (per link created or per document sent) maps better to episodic sales cadences.

What pricing structure did you land on for your tool? Curious whether you saw the same seat-size dynamics.
