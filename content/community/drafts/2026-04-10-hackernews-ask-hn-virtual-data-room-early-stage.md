## Context

A first-time founder raising a seed round asked HN what tools founders actually use for data rooms vs. what advisors recommend (which apparently is always "Intralinks" or "Datasite" — enterprise tools that cost $25K+). Thread has practical answers from founders and angels. This is a knowledge-sharing thread where experienced founders share what actually works at seed/pre-seed. Peeeky can be mentioned as a lightweight alternative with natural context.

Confidence is 3: Thread is a general question, not a focused pitch-deck-tracking thread. Response can be helpful but Peeeky mention should be soft and one of several options.

## Proposed Response

At seed/pre-seed, a data room doesn't need to be Intralinks. Here's what actually works:

**Google Drive or Notion** (most common for pre-seed): Shared folder, access-revocable, free. The downside is zero visibility — you don't know who opened what, and you can't tell if your lead investor actually reviewed the financial model or just opened the folder once.

**DocSend** (the default for pitch decks): Page-level analytics and email gate are genuinely useful. The $45–85/month price point is reasonable for a founder actively raising. The analytics are investor-deck focused, not data-room optimized.

**Papermark** (open-source alternative): Free tier is generous, analytics are solid. Good for teams who want to self-host or want no vendor lock-in.

**Lighter tools for deck-only** (Peeeky and a couple others): Some founders use these when they want analytics + the ability for investors to ask the deck questions via AI, which reduces back-and-forth on basic "can you clarify X" questions.

The real question at seed stage is: do you need the full due diligence room now, or are you still in the "send the deck to 50 VCs" phase? For the latter, a proper data room is premature — use something light with tracking and upgrade when a VC asks for diligence materials.
