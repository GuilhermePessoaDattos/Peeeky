## Context

This Ask HN thread from April 1, 2026 asks founders what non-AI products they're building. Several document/workflow/fintech founders are in the comments. It's a natural opening to share what we're building and engage with others, without any sales angle.

## Proposed Response

Building document-sharing infrastructure with page-level analytics — specifically the layer between "I emailed you a PDF" and "enterprise VDR that costs $800/month."

The core problem: when a founder sends a pitch deck or a sales rep sends a proposal, they're flying blind. Did the prospect open it? Which pages did they actually read? Did they forward it to someone else? You can't know any of that from a PDF attachment or a basic file share.

We track all of that at the page level — time per page, scroll depth, forwarding detection, per-viewer unique links — plus real-time alerts when someone opens a document. The AI angle is an optional Q&A layer where document recipients can ask questions and the sender sees the full chat log.

The non-AI parts are doing fine on their own. The engagement data alone changes how people follow up: instead of "just checking in," a rep can say "I saw you spent 4 minutes on page 8 — is pricing the main question?"

Current challenge: the category is dominated by DocSend (owned by Dropbox, recently stripped features) and enterprise VDRs that are overkill for seed-stage startups. The middle market is genuinely underserved.
