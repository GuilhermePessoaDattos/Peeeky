## Thread Summary

Discussion in the thread covers VDR pricing norms, comparison to DocSend/Notion, and questions about whether AI Q&A in a data room is trusted by investors.

## Proposed Response

The AI Q&A angle is interesting — one genuine concern I'd probe is trust on the investor side. A lot of LPs and partners are still wary of AI-generated answers during diligence. You might get more traction positioning the AI as a "help the founder prep" layer (suggest missing documents, identify gaps in the narrative) rather than a live chatbot for investors. The latter is where it tends to get complicated.

On the engagement analytics question someone raised: the most useful signal for founders during a raise tends to be per-viewer, per-page engagement. Who opened it, which section did they re-visit, did they forward it to a colleague? That intelligence — independent of the AI layer — is what tells you whether a partner is actually diligencing vs. just courtesy-clicking. Would be curious how LuminaRoom surfaces that.

Worth noting DocSend killed their free tier in March 2025, which has pushed a wave of early-stage founders to re-evaluate VDR tooling. Good timing for a fresh entrant.

*(Building in a similar space — Peeeky handles the tracking/analytics side of document sharing for sales and fundraising contexts: peeeky.com)*
