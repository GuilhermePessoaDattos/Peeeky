## Proposed Response

Nice scope here — the search-inside-documents problem is genuinely hard to do well, especially when you want query semantics rather than just keyword matching.

One thing I'd be curious about: do you surface *who* queried what, or when? That access log layer turns out to matter a lot in some workflows — particularly when documents are being shared externally (sales decks, investor materials, due diligence packages). The "what did they read" question is often as important as "can I find what I need."

The dashboard angle is interesting. We've been building on the sender side of this problem — giving the person who shares a document visibility into how recipients engage with it (which pages, how long, how many views). Very different surface but adjacent intent: making document engagement legible.

What's the primary persona you're targeting — internal teams searching their own docs, or something more external-facing?

*(Disclosure: building Peeeky — peeeky.com — which lives on the sharing/analytics side of this space.)*
