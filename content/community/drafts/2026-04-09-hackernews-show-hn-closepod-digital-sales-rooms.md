A B2B sales room product launch thread. This is a perfect thread for Peeeky — sales reps are clearly shopping, the DocSend-too-expensive pain is live in the thread, and AI document chat for proposals is a differentiator no one in the thread mentioned.

## Proposed Response

The pricing critique in this thread is real — DocSend's per-user model becomes painful the moment you're scaling an SDR team. The per-seat cost structure made sense when document sharing was a solo-founder fundraising tool; it breaks down badly for sales teams where 10 reps each need to send tracked proposals.

The UX complexity point is also well-taken. The best signal we've seen for a sales doc tool's adoption rate: can a new SDR send a tracked proposal link in under 60 seconds on day one without any onboarding? If it takes a training session, you've lost the rep.

On the analytics side — one underrated use case for sales: knowing not just *that* a prospect opened a proposal but *which section they re-read three times* is often more actionable than close-date fields in the CRM. That's the conversation-starter for the follow-up call. If recipients can also ask the document questions directly via AI chat, the engagement data gets even richer (you see what they were uncertain about).
