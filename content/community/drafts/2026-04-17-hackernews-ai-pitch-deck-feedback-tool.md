## Proposed Response

Improving the deck is the right starting point — but there's an underrated second problem: knowing whether the deck is actually working in the wild. You can optimize the narrative, but if you're sending it as an email attachment or a plain PDF link, you're still flying blind.

What we've found talking to founders in the middle of a raise: the most actionable signal isn't "does my deck have a strong problem slide" (important, but you figure that out once) — it's "which slides are investors spending time on, which are they skipping, and are they coming back for a second look." That's a runtime feedback loop the deck-creation tools don't cover.

A natural extension for your tool: let the founder share via a tracked link right from your interface, then surface engagement data as a second feedback channel. "Investors are dropping off at slide 8" is more specific than any AI critique.
