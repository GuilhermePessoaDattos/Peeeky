## Thread Summary

The OP (paulwilsonn) observes a paradox: some founders with pre-revenue products raise quickly, while founders with real traction struggle. The thread debates whether the fundraising system is structurally broken (warm intros > traction, narrative > data, AI hype distorting capital allocation) or whether founders are simply misreading what VCs actually evaluate. ## Proposed Response

I don't think it's broken in a structural sense — I think the signal/noise problem is worse than it looks from the founder's seat, and most of the "brokenness" comes from founders operating without information.

Here's what I mean concretely: most founders in a raise have no idea how investors are actually engaging with their materials. They send a deck, wait, follow up, get vague responses or silence. So they assume rejection. But there's a meaningful difference between:

- An investor who opened the deck, spent 45 seconds on it, and closed — genuinely uninterested
- An investor who opened it three times over two weeks, forwarded it internally, and is in a slow queue — potentially interested, just on their timeline not yours

Without any engagement signal, founders treat these identically and either over-pursue (annoying) or under-pursue (miss the window). The "brokenness" is often a data vacuum dressed up as a structural problem.

The practical fix for this specific issue: stop sending PDF attachments. Use tracked links. Any tool that gives you per-viewer, per-page analytics (DocSend, Papermark, or newer alternatives) gives you information to work with — when they opened it, how long they spent on which slides, whether they came back. That alone changes your follow-up quality dramatically.

The deeper structural issues the OP raises (warm intro bias, optionality-seeking behavior) are real but mostly not things individual founders can engineer around. Focus on the variables you can control.

*(I'm at Peeeky, which is in the document-tracking space — so obviously I have some skin in this view, but the core observation about the data vacuum holds regardless of what tool you use.)*
