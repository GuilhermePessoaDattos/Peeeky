## Proposed Response

The heatmap-per-slide concept is genuinely useful, but I'd push back slightly on FOMO overlays with social proof messaging in an investor context. Founders use DocSend-style tools partly because it signals seriousness and professionalism to VCs — a "12 other investors are viewing this deck" ticker might read as gimmicky to a partner at a serious fund and could undermine credibility at the exact moment you need it.

The pitfall I'd watch most: investors who know they're being tracked sometimes close tabs quickly to avoid signaling interest too early. You may end up with systematically short session times on the most engaged prospects. Pairing slide-time data with *return visits* (did they come back a second or third time?) tends to be a stronger buying signal than raw time-on-slide.

The AI chat angle — letting the investor ask the deck questions directly — is something worth layering on top of heatmaps. It closes the loop between "they spent 90 seconds on the traction slide" and "here are the three questions they actually had about it." That's the data that makes a follow-up call land differently.
