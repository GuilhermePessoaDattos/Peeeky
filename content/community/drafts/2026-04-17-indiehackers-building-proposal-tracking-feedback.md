## Proposed Response

This was the exact problem we kept running into when talking to early-stage founders — the follow-up timing problem. You send a deck, silence. You can't tell if they opened it, skimmed it, or never looked. So you either follow up too early (annoying) or too late (momentum gone).

The clearest signal we found: **don't send attachments or plain links, send a tracked link**. The difference between "sent a PDF" and "sent a trackable link" is the difference between flying blind and knowing:
- Did they open it at all?
- Which slides did they spend time on?
- Did they come back for a second view?
- Did someone else on their team look at it?

That last one — forwarding — is huge. When a VP of Sales opens your deck and then their CEO looks at it 20 minutes later, that's a buying signal. A plain email gives you nothing.

For tools: Papermark is free and open source if you want to self-host. We built Peeeky for exactly this — tracked links, per-page analytics, AI chat for viewers, access controls. Free plan covers 5 documents. peeeky.com if you want to try it.

The follow-up rule I use: if they've viewed the deck but haven't replied, follow up within 24 hours while it's still fresh. If they haven't opened it after 5 days, try a different touch (call, different format).
