## Proposed Response

Congrats on the $42K milestone — that's real validation that the category has legs beyond DocSend's pricing frustrations.

Building in a similar space (Peeeky — peeeky.com) and a few things we've been noticing that might be useful:

**The customer split is interesting:**

We see two very different use cases attracting users. The first is fundraising/investor decks — founders who want to know if an investor actually read slides 8-12 or just skimmed the cover. The second is B2B sales — reps who want to know if their proposal made it past the gatekeeper to the actual decision-maker. The product requirements overlap heavily, but the urgency and workflow context are quite different. Curious if Papermark sees the same split.

**On the data room side:**

There's a cohort of users who start with single-document tracking and graduate to wanting a full folder structure with per-viewer permissions for due diligence. That "graduation" seems to happen around Series A prep or when a company is doing M&A. The enterprise VDR players (Ansarada, Datasite) leave a lot of room in that mid-market gap.

**What we've found differentiates:**

Real-time alerts (within 60 seconds of a document being opened) are the feature that converts skeptics. The "did they open it" question is what founders actually want answered, and anything with a 10-minute delay feels broken. Getting that pipeline fast and reliable has been worth more than any feature addition.

Good luck with the next milestone — would be curious to chat sometime about what we're each seeing in the market.
