After looking at thousands of document views through Peeeky, here is what we found:

The average shared document gets opened within 3.2 hours of being sent. But 41% of recipients never open it at all.

### Post 5: Educational - Pitch Deck Mistake
**Best day to post:** Wednesday
**Hashtags:** #FounderTips #PitchDeck #Fundraising

---

The number one mistake founders make when sharing their pitch deck is treating every investor the same.

You have one PDF. You send it to 50 investors. Same deck, same email, same follow-up cadence. And then you wonder why your response rate is under 10%.

Here is what the best fundraisers do differently.

First, they track engagement. They know exactly who opened the deck, how long they spent on it, and which slides held their attention. They use that data to segment investors into hot, warm, and cold.

Second, they customize follow-ups based on behavior. If someone spent 4 minutes on your market size slide, your follow-up should lead with market data. If they skipped straight to financials, lead with your numbers.

Third, they set expiration dates on shared links. This creates urgency and lets you control which version of your deck is circulating.

Fourth, they never send a naked PDF attachment. A trackable link gives you data. An attachment gives you nothing.

We built Peeeky to make all of this easy. Track, analyze, and follow up with precision. No more guessing who is interested.

Start for free at peeeky.com. Your fundraise will thank you.

### Post 8: Feature Highlight - Engagement Scoring
**Best day to post:** Tuesday
**Hashtags:** #SalesIntelligence #Analytics #B2B

---

Not all document views are equal. Here is how to tell who is actually interested.

Someone opens your proposal and closes it after 8 seconds. Someone else spends 14 minutes, revisits the pricing page three times, and forwards it to a colleague.

These are not the same level of interest. But if all you have is an "opened" notification, they look identical.

That is why we built engagement scoring into Peeeky.

Every recipient gets a score based on real behavior. Time spent reading. Pages viewed. Pages revisited. Whether they scrolled to the end. Whether they returned for a second session. Whether they forwarded the link.

High scores mean genuine interest. Low scores mean polite curiosity at best.

This changes how you prioritize follow-ups entirely.

Instead of working through your prospect list top to bottom, you work it by engagement score. The person who spent 20 minutes deep in your technical documentation gets a call today. The person who bounced after the cover page gets a nurture email next week.

Your sales team stops wasting time on dead leads and focuses energy where it matters.

Why we built a free DocSend alternative.

I have nothing against DocSend. It is a solid product that proved document tracking matters. But after Dropbox acquired it, the pricing went up and the innovation slowed down.

A lot of founders and sales teams I talked to said the same things. It is too expensive for what it does. The free plan is basically useless. There is no AI. The analytics could be deeper.

So we built Peeeky with a few clear principles.

One: the free plan should be genuinely useful. Not a teaser that forces you to upgrade before you get any value.

Two: AI should be baked in, not bolted on. Document chat, smart summaries, and engagement insights powered by AI are part of the core product.

Three: pricing should make sense for startups. $39/mo for Pro. $129/mo for Business. Not $250/seat/month.

Four: data rooms, eSignature, watermarks, and email verification should all be in one platform. You should not need four tools to share a document securely.

### Post 10: Retrospective / Learnings
**Best day to post:** Thursday
**Hashtags:** #StartupLessons #LaunchDay #BuildingInPublic

---

Here is what we learned launching Peeeky publicly.

We soft-launched three months ago and just completed our public launch. Some of these lessons were expensive. Sharing them so yours do not have to be.

Lesson 1: Your first users do not care about your feature list. They care about one thing. For us, that was "show me who opened my document." Everything else came later.

Lesson 2: Free plans drive adoption but not feedback. Our best feedback came from the first 20 paying customers, not the first 200 free users. Paying customers tell you what is broken because they expect it to work.

Lesson 3: The feature you think is your differentiator is not what users talk about. We thought AI chat would be our headline. Users kept raving about per-page analytics. Build what they love, not what you love.

Lesson 4: Pricing is positioning. When we said "free DocSend alternative," we got tire-kickers. When we said "document intelligence platform," we got decision-makers.

Lesson 5: Launch is not a moment. It is a process. Our "launch day" was actually a 3-week campaign. The LinkedIn post was just one touchpoint.

We are still early. Still learning. If you want to follow along or try the product, visit peeeky.com.

And if you have launch stories of your own, drop them in the comments. I read every one.
